# Event Policies & Terms of Participation

*Last Updated: April 24, 2026*

By entering and participating in the DA Line Follower Robot Contest at De Anza College, all participants and attendees agree to the following terms and conditions.

## 1. Liability Waiver & Hold Harmless
Participants assume all risks associated with the event. The event organizers, De Anza College, and the Foothill-De Anza Community College District (FHDA) are not liable or responsible for:
- Any personal injury sustained during the event.
- Property damage, including but not limited to broken robots, blown electronics, or damaged tools.
- Lost or stolen property.

*Note: All participants will be required to sign a physical paper liability waiver at the door upon check-in prior to competing.*

## 2. Facility & Safety Guidelines (De Anza Campus)
To comply with campus safety standards:
- **Battery Safety:** Only safe charging practices are permitted. Charging damaged or swollen Lithium Polymer (LiPo) batteries is strictly prohibited. Never leave charging batteries unattended. **All LiPo charging must take place at the designated charging station and/or utilize fire-safe LiPo charging bags.**
- **Venue Rules:** No food or open drink containers are allowed directly on or near the competition tracks or pit areas.
- **Track & Floor Care:** All tracks are constructed using low-tack, floor-safe tape (e.g., painter's or specialized electrical tape) to ensure no residue is left behind and De Anza College floor finishes remain protected.
- **Compliance:** Attendees must adhere to all current De Anza College and Santa Clara County health and safety guidelines.

## 3. Minors Policy
For participants under the age of 18 (e.g., middle school and high school students):
- You must be accompanied by a parent, legal guardian, or designated school chaperone at all times.
- Your liability waiver and media release must be signed by your parent or legal guardian.

## 4. Code of Conduct & Anti-Discrimination (Title IX)
In accordance with California Community Colleges Title 5 and Title IX regulations:
- We are committed to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, race, or religion.
- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind will not be tolerated. 
- Violators of this Code of Conduct will be immediately disqualified and removed from the campus venue.

## 5. Media & Photography Release
By attending this public event, you consent to being photographed, filmed, and/or recorded. The organizers reserve the right to use these media assets for promotional purposes, social media, and future event marketing without compensation. If you have specific privacy concerns, please contact the organizers upon check-in.

## 6. ADA Accessibility
We are committed to making this event accessible to all. If you require reasonable accommodations (e.g., modified pit tables, track access assistance) per the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) or California's Unruh Civil Rights Act, please contact the organizers prior to the event date.
